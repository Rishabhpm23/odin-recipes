Garlic bread

Description

Garlic bread is a popular and flavorful side dish or appetizer characterized by slices of bread, typically baguette or French bread, generously spread with a mixture of butter, minced garlic, and often complemented with herbs like parsley. The bread is then toasted or baked until golden and crispy, allowing the garlic-infused butter to create a deliciously aromatic and savory experience. Served warm, garlic bread is a versatile accompaniment to pasta dishes, soups, or enjoyed on its own as a tasty and comforting treat.

Ingredients

  • Baguette or French bread
  • Butter
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Parsley
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to around 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine softened butter with minced garlic. Optionally, add grated Parmesan and chopped parsley for extra flavor.
  3. Cut the baguette or French bread into even slices, ensuring not to cut all the way through.
  4. Generously spread the garlic butter mixture onto each slice, making sure to cover both sides.
  5. Close the bread loaf if you had sliced through it, and wrap it in aluminum foil.
  6. Place the wrapped bread in the preheated oven for about 10-15 minutes, or until the bread is heated through and the butter is melted.
  7. For a crispy top, open the foil and broil for an additional 1-2 minutes until the top is golden brown. Keep a close eye to prevent burning.
  8. Unwrap the garlic bread, slice if necessary, and serve warm. Enjoy the aromatic and flavorful treat!
